Academic Research

Research Work

Pandemic-Era Shifts in Drug-Substance Co-occurrence and Mental Illness Risk (2020–2024)

This project carries out an in-depth exploratory data analysis and statistical modeling on five years of national public health survey data (2020–2024), looking at how substance use — tobacco, alcohol, and illicit drugs — relates to mental health outcomes like Any Mental Illness, Serious Mental Illness, and Major Depressive Episode. The work combines unweighted sample statistics with survey-weighted analyses to produce population-level estimates that account for complex survey designs.

The pipeline covers everything from data ingestion and cross-wave harmonization to logistic regression models with year interaction terms, forest plots of adjusted odds ratios, and likelihood ratio tests to validate temporal dynamics. Built entirely in Python using pandas, statsmodels, matplotlib, and scipy.

Python statsmodels GLM Survey Weighting Logistic Regression EDA NSDUH Data
Women's Civic Participation & Community Philanthropy in Ghana

Using Afrobarometer Round 9 nationally representative survey data (N = 2,369), this study examines the structural barriers shaping women's participation in community-based philanthropic organizations in Ghana. Three survey-weighted logistic regression models test whether material deprivation, gender, residential context, and educational attainment predict active civic participation.

The findings reveal a significant gender gap — women are 55% less likely than men to be active community organization members (OR = 0.45, p < .001), even after controlling for poverty, education, and residence. Rural respondents are nearly three times more likely to participate than urban counterparts (OR = 2.74, p < .001), pointing to community organizations as informal safety nets where state services are weakest. The analysis draws on Layton's framework of community philanthropy, Sen's capability approach, and Mullainathan and Shafir's bandwidth-tax framework.

R Logistic Regression Survey Weighting Afrobarometer Gender Equity West Africa
